Synthesis

What the source pack is optimized to teach

Start with choreography, not theory

The room maps are the clearest organizing backbone in the folder. They define where anesthesia, surgeon, scrub tech, assistant, monitors, back tables, and both c-arm positions belong before anyone reaches for the procedural documents.

Treat imaging as its own training module

The c-arm section is large and deliberately segmented, with dedicated clips for terminology, AP setup, lateral setup, and additional setup footage. That suggests imaging workflow deserves focused onboarding rather than a quick footnote.

Keep setup, documentation, and care linked

The folder does not stop at intra-op references. It also includes coding, an op note example, consent, pain protocols, tapering guidance, and post-op nerve root irritation material, which makes the packet feel operational end to end.

Training Path

Use the materials in this order

This sequence mirrors how the shared folder is most useful in practice: room first, imaging next, then the working table, core references, post-op support, and research backup.

01

Room map and staff positions

Review the basic, advanced, and open-room diagrams to lock in physical placement of staff, monitors, c-arm paths, mayo table, and back tables.

02

C-arm terminology and setup videos

Move from still diagrams into the shared c-arm clips. The folder separates terminology and AP work from lateral setup, which makes it ideal for orientation drills.

03

Mayo stand and preference card alignment

Cross-check the mayo stand photos with the preference card so the table layout and item list are being learned together instead of in isolation.

04

Core procedural references

Keep the STG, IFU, 510(k), coding summary, and op note example clustered as the main document stack for case prep, compliance, and documentation.

05

Peri-op and post-op protocols

Use the care-protocol packet for consent, conservative protocol context, pain management, tapering logic, and postoperative nerve root irritation references.

06

Evidence and talking points

Finish with the white paper library when the audience needs published support, outcomes framing, radiation discussion, BMI context, scoliosis material, or landmark-study references.
